Nine things we changed

What actually makes it feel premium.

None of these need new features — they're token-level changes to colour, type, shadow, and rhythm. The data is identical between the two phones above.

  1. A human greeting."Evening, Amelia" in Playfair italic, with her initial in a gold badge. Sets the tone in one line.
  2. Stat cards that don't overlap.Clean 4-column grid with tabular-nums, gold unit marks, top-edge gloss, proper 14px padding. No more status-bar bleed.
  3. Gold accent replaces purple.The Sleep Coach callout now belongs to the palette. Rose or gold — never purple.
  4. Editorial section headings."Today, at a glance" — Playfair italic throughout. Says "journal", not "spreadsheet". (Parisienne is reserved for the OBubba wordmark alone.)
  5. Time as the primary column.Left-aligned time on its own, in gold. The eye scans down the column like a diary, not left-to-right like a CSV row.
  6. One featured row per screen.The long nap gets a gold-edged card so your eye lands. Everything else steps back.
  7. Warmer night palette.Deep midnight with candlelit gold spills — not clinical navy + pure white. Like reading by a bedside lamp.
  8. Softer icon treatment.Circled emoji with a faint gold ring, not a harsh white border. Reads as jewellery, not a button.
  9. Tab bar that recedes.Active tab is a gold glow + Playfair italic label — no hard rectangle around every icon. Bottom of the screen gets quiet.
